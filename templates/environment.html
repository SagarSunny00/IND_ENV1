Key Components of Environmental Management

Environmental Policy

A statement of an organization's commitment to environmental management and continuous improvement. It outlines the principles and objectives that guide environmental decision-making.

  • Mission statement and environmental vision
  • Specific commitments to regulatory compliance
  • Resource conservation principles
  • Framework for setting environmental objectives

Environmental Impact Assessment

A systematic process to identify, predict, and evaluate the environmental effects of proposed actions or projects. It helps in making informed decisions about environmental impacts.

  • Identifying potential environmental impacts
  • Evaluating significance of impacts
  • Developing mitigation measures
  • Monitoring and auditing requirements

Compliance and Monitoring

Procedures and systems to ensure operations comply with environmental regulations and to monitor environmental performance metrics.

  • Regulatory tracking systems
  • Environmental measurement programs
  • Regular compliance audits
  • Corrective action plans

Training and Awareness

Programs to educate employees about environmental policies, procedures, and their role in environmental management.

  • Employee environmental training
  • Contractor awareness programs
  • Role-specific environmental training
  • Communication of environmental performance

Environmental Management Standards & Certifications

ISO 14001

The international standard for environmental management systems (EMS). It provides a framework that organizations can follow to establish an effective EMS.

85% of Fortune 500 companies are ISO 14001 certified

EMAS (Eco-Management and Audit Scheme)

A premium management instrument developed by the European Commission for organizations to evaluate, report, and improve their environmental performance.

42% adoption rate among European corporations

Carbon Trust Standard

Recognizes organizations for measuring, managing, and reducing their carbon footprint and resource usage.

55% increase in adoption over the past 5 years

Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED)

A green building certification program focused on building design, construction, and operation.

70% of new commercial buildings seek LEED certification

Implementing an Environmental Management System

Implementation Process

  1. Initial Environmental Review: Assess current environmental impacts and practices.
  2. Environmental Policy Development: Create a policy that reflects commitment to environmental management.
  3. Planning: Identify environmental aspects, legal requirements, and set objectives and targets.
  4. Implementation: Establish responsibilities, training, documentation, and operational controls.
  5. Checking and Corrective Action: Monitor and measure performance, conduct audits, and implement corrective actions.
  6. Management Review: Executive review of the EMS to ensure its continuing suitability and effectiveness.
  7. Continuous Improvement: Ongoing refinement of the system based on performance data and changing conditions.
